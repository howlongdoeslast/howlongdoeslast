  ## How long does fried rice last in the fridge?


  Fried rice can stay fresh for up to 7 days in a refrigerator if stored with the proper method. It can be stored both in an airtight container or tightly wrapped in a plastic bag. If you want your fried rice to last longer, then you can freeze it. Frozen fried rice will be good for up to 2 months.\

  When it comes to reheating the fried rice, make sure that it is piping hot throughout before serving. It is best to reheat in a wok or frying pan over high heat.


  ## How to tell if fried rice is gone bad?


  The first thing you’ll notice with bad fried rice is the smell. If your fried rice has an off odor, it’s probably time to ditch it. Another way to tell if your fried rice is no longer good is by looking at the color. If the rice has turned a shade of brown or gray, it’s time to toss it. Finally, if the fried rice is sticky or clumpy, it’s a sign that it’s gone bad.


  ## How to store fried rice properly?


  Fried rice is a popular dish that can be made with leftover rice or fresh rice. The dish can include vegetables, meat, and sauces. Fried rice can last for 5-7 days in the fridge if it is stored properly. Place it in an airtight container or sealable bag to store fried rice. Ensure to refrigerate the rice as soon as possible after it is cooked. Fried rice can also be frozen for 2-3 months.


  To reheat fried rice, place it in a microwave-safe container and heat it on high for 2-3 minutes. Alternatively, the rice can be heated in a skillet over medium heat until warm.


  ## What kind of meat is used in fried rice?


  The most common meats used in fried rice are chicken, pork, and beef. However, other meats such as shrimp, crabmeat, and tofu can also be used. The type of meat used is typically determined by the cuisine or region of the world where the dish originated. For example, beef is commonly used in southern Chinese cuisine, while **[chicken](https://howlongdoeslast.info/chicken-in-fridge/)** is more common in northern **[Chinese cuisine](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Chinese_cuisine)**.
