## How to know if Bose speakers are gone bad?

The most common symptom of a bad or failing speaker is a blown speaker. A blown speaker will usually produce a popping sound followed by a hissing noise and will not be able to produce any audio. In some cases, the speaker may still be able to produce sound, but it will be tinny and lack any bass. If you are experiencing any of these issues, it is likely that your speaker has gone bad and needs to be replaced.

There are several other ways to tell if your Bose speakers have gone bad. One is to check the speaker’s wiring. If the wiring is loose or frayed, the speaker may not work properly. Another way to check is to test the speaker’s resistance. To do this, you will need a multimeter. Resistance should be between 4 and 8 ohms for most Bose speakers. The speaker is likely bad if the resistance is outside of this range. Finally, you can also check the speaker’s voice coil for damage.
