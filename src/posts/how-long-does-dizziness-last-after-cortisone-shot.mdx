## How Long Does Dizziness Last After Cortisone Shot?

Normally, a Cortisone shot creates dizziness that can last for a few minutes. In most cases, the **dizziness can last for 15 minutes or less**. It is advised that you should not drive, operate heavy machinery, or do any type of dangerous activity. And if the dizziness lasts more than 15 minutes after receiving a shot, then there are other problems that you should be concerned with.

## What is Cortisone?

Cortisone is a steroid hormone produced in the **[adrenal glands](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Adrenal_gland)**. It has many effects on the body, often give relieving from inflammation. Although it can be taken orally, injected cortisones are more effective because they have direct access to the bloodstream without being broken down by enzymes first. Over time, Cortisone can cause a lot of damage because it suppresses the immune system. Many people who take Cortisone over a long period of time often experience extreme side effects such as weakened bones, obesity, and diabetes. However, if taken in therapeutic dosages for short periods of time, Cortisone can be effective and safe.

## How Long Does It Take for Cortisone to Work in Body?

Cortisone starts working in your body after four to five days. In some cases, the drug may take a few weeks to work. So if you feel dizziness after a cortisone shot, then it will continue to affect you for at least three to five days after injection.

In some cases, after a cortisone shot in the shoulder or knees, patients may experience the symptoms of carpal tunnel syndrome, which can occur after 5 to 10 days of injection.

The half-life of Cortisone injection is the time it takes for the body to process and eliminate 50% of the drug. So if you take a shot, then after 1 hour or 90 minutes, only 50% of Cortisone will be eliminated from your system; after two hours (180 minutes), 25%, etc.
